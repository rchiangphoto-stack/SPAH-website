What to Expect at Your First Visit

Walking into a new animal hospital can feel overwhelming, but at South Pasadena Animal Hospital, we make sure every first visit starts with a warm, stress-free welcome. Our front desk team will greet you and your pet by name and help you get settled while we review any medical records you've brought along.

Your veterinarian will then conduct a thorough nose-to-tail physical examination, taking the time to check your pet's weight, heart, lungs, teeth, skin, and overall condition. We'll discuss your pet's medical history, diet, behavior, and lifestyle so we can build a personalized care plan that fits both your pet's needs and your budget.

Whether you're bringing in a puppy for their first vaccinations, a senior cat for a checkup, or a bearded dragon for a wellness exam, our team treats every animal with patience and genuine compassion. Before you leave, we'll walk you through our recommendations, answer every question, and make sure you feel confident about the next steps.

Animal Hospital Alhambra FAQ

What services does your animal hospital offer?

We offer comprehensive veterinary care including wellness exams, vaccinations, dental cleanings, soft tissue surgery (including spay/neuter), in-house bloodwork and lab testing, digital X-rays, skin allergy treatment, and care for exotic pets. Visit our services page for the full list.

Do you perform surgery?

Yes. We perform soft tissue surgical procedures including spay/neuter, mass removals, and other common surgeries. All procedures are performed with careful anesthesia monitoring. For complex orthopedic or advanced surgical cases, we can provide referrals to a surgical center.

Do you offer emergency or urgent care?

We do our best to accommodate urgent cases during business hours (Monday–Friday, 8 AM–6 PM). If your pet needs urgent attention, call us at (626) 441-1314 and we'll work to see them as quickly as possible. For after-hours emergencies, we recommend contacting a 24-hour emergency animal hospital in the area.

Do you treat exotic pets?

Yes. We treat a wide range of exotic animals including reptiles (bearded dragons, geckos, turtles), birds (cockatiels, parrots, finches), rabbits, guinea pigs, hamsters, and other small mammals. We are one of the few animal hospitals in the Alhambra area equipped to care for non-traditional pets. Visit our exotic vet page for more.

How much does a visit cost?

Our service prices are published on our pricing page so you can plan ahead. We believe in transparent pricing with no surprise charges, and we'll always discuss costs with you before proceeding with any diagnostics or treatment.

Do you have in-house diagnostics?

Yes. Our in-house laboratory allows us to run bloodwork, urinalysis, and other diagnostic tests with same-day results — often within minutes. We also have digital X-ray capabilities for imaging. This means faster answers and quicker treatment for your pet.
