Why Groom Your Cat at a Veterinary Clinic

Built for Cats Who Won't Tolerate a Groom

Plenty of cats simply will not sit for a bath or a shave, and forcing it isn't safe or kind. With a veterinary team on-site, badly matted or fearful cats can be groomed the right way, including with sedation when a veterinarian determines it's appropriate — an option a standalone salon can't offer.

What's Hiding Under the Coat

Shaving down a matted cat often reveals what the fur was hiding — skin sores, lumps, fleas, wounds. Because our groomers work alongside our veterinarians, anything concerning can be looked at the same day rather than sent home unaddressed.

Gentle on Senior Cats

Older cats stop grooming themselves well, and their skin gets thin and delicate. We handle senior cats slowly and carefully, with the reassurance of medical support close by if a cat gets overwhelmed or a health issue surfaces mid-visit.

When a Cat Stops Grooming Itself

Cats are meticulous self-groomers, so when one stops keeping up with its own coat, it usually means something has changed. Overweight cats can't physically reach their back and rear. Arthritic and senior cats find the twisting painful. And a cat that suddenly lets itself go can be telling you it doesn't feel well. The matting that follows isn't just cosmetic — tight mats pull on the skin, trap moisture and dirt, and can hide sores underneath.

This is where grooming and medicine overlap, and why doing it inside a clinic makes sense. A severely matted cat sometimes needs to be sedated to be shaved safely, and that's a decision for a veterinarian, not something a salon can do. If your cat is matted, overweight, elderly, or has simply stopped grooming, tell us when you reach out — it helps us plan the visit and, if needed, loop in our veterinary team. You can learn more about our full range of veterinary services for cats as well.

Cat Grooming FAQ

How much does cat grooming cost in Alhambra?

It depends on your cat's coat, the degree of matting, the service, and whether a veterinarian recommends sedation for safety — so we give you a quote for your specific cat rather than a flat rate. Call (626) 441-1314 or reach out here and we'll walk you through it before you book.

Do cats really need professional grooming?

Many short-haired cats groom themselves well and rarely need help. But long-haired breeds, overweight cats, senior cats, and any cat that has stopped grooming itself often do — matting causes real discomfort and can hide skin problems. If you're not sure whether your cat needs it, ask us and we'll give you an honest answer.

My cat is badly matted and won't hold still — what are my options?

This is one of the most common reasons cat owners come to us. A severely matted cat sometimes can't be shaved safely while awake, and because we're a veterinary clinic, our veterinarians can evaluate your cat and, when appropriate, use sedation so the groom can be done calmly and without injury. That's something a standalone salon isn't able to offer.

Do you groom senior cats?

Yes, and it's an area where a veterinary setting really helps. Older cats have delicate skin and often other health considerations, so we work slowly and keep our veterinary team close in case anything comes up. Let us know your cat's age and any conditions when you book.

How often should a cat be groomed?

Long-haired cats often benefit from a groom every few months to stay ahead of matting, while many short-haired cats need little more than occasional nail trims. We'll suggest a rhythm based on your cat's coat and how well they keep up with self-grooming.

Can I combine grooming with a vet visit?

Yes. Combining a groom with a wellness exam saves your cat a second stressful trip, and it means anything noticed during grooming can be checked by the veterinary team the same day. Mention you'd like both when you call.
