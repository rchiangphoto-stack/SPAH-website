Cat Vet Near Pasadena — FAQ

How far is SPAH from Pasadena?

About 10 minutes via the 110 freeway south. We're at 3116 W Main St in Alhambra with free street parking out front.

What vaccines does my cat need?

Core cat vaccines include FVRCP (feline viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia) and rabies. FeLV (feline leukemia) is recommended for cats with outdoor access. We'll tailor the vaccine schedule to your cat's age and lifestyle at their wellness exam.

Do you treat senior cats?

Yes. Senior cats (typically 10 years and older) benefit from more frequent exams and bloodwork to monitor kidney function, thyroid levels, blood pressure, and other age-related conditions. Early detection makes a significant difference in long-term outcomes.

Do you treat hyperthyroidism in cats?

Yes. Hyperthyroidism is one of the most common conditions in senior cats. We diagnose it through bloodwork and manage it with medication or dietary therapy, tailored to your cat's overall health and your preferences.

Dr. Sylvia Chiang and Dr. Gina Navia lead an independent practice where cats receive unhurried, attentive care without the assembly-line feel of corporate clinics.

Dental disease is one of the most common yet overlooked health issues in cats. Over 70% of cats show signs of dental disease by age three. We offer professional cleanings and dental X-rays to catch problems early, as untreated dental disease can affect the kidneys, heart, and liver over time.

For Pasadena cat owners with senior cats, we recommend twice-yearly wellness visits and annual bloodwork starting around age 10. Conditions like hyperthyroidism, chronic kidney disease, and hypertension are highly manageable when detected early. View our pricing or book an appointment today.
