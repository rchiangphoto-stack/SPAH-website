Why Groom Your Dog at a Veterinary Clinic

A Veterinary Team On-Site

If something comes up mid-groom — a skin issue, an ear that looks infected, a dog who gets overwhelmed — there's a veterinary team right here, not a phone call away. That safety net is the whole point of grooming inside a clinic.

The Same Team That Knows Your Dog

When your dog is already a patient here, the people bathing and clipping them can see the medical history — the allergy flare-ups, the arthritic hips, the spot that's sensitive to the touch. Grooming and health care stop being two separate worlds.

Groom and Wellness in One Trip

Pair a grooming appointment with a wellness exam and your dog handles one trip instead of two. It's easier on nervous dogs, easier on your schedule, and it means we can catch things during a groom that turn into a same-day conversation with the vet.

Especially for Dogs Who Find Grooming Hard

Some dogs sail through a groom. Others don't — and those are the dogs a veterinary setting is built for. A senior dog with arthritis who can't stand comfortably for a long session, a nervous rescue who shuts down on a salon table, a bulldog or pug whose flat face makes drying and handling more delicate, a dog with a heart or breathing condition that a standalone groomer may not feel equipped to manage. For all of them, having a veterinary team in the building changes what's possible.

We take these dogs at a pace that suits them, and we stop when we need to rather than pushing through. If your dog has a medical condition, or has had a bad grooming experience before, tell us when you book — that context shapes how we plan the visit. And if your dog's anxiety runs deep, our veterinarians can talk with you about calming options as part of the same visit. You can read more about that on our dog anxiety guide.

Dog Grooming FAQ

How much does dog grooming cost in Alhambra?

Grooming prices depend on your dog's size, coat type, condition, and the style you want, so we give you a quote rather than a one-size-fits-all number. Call us at (626) 441-1314 or reach out here and we'll walk you through pricing for your specific dog before you book.

What makes grooming at a vet different from a regular salon?

The difference is the safety net. If your dog gets anxious, a nail is quicked, or the groomer notices a skin lump, ear infection, or dental problem, there's a veterinary team in the same building. For most healthy dogs a good salon is fine — but for anxious, senior, or medically fragile dogs, having medical support on-site matters.

Do you groom anxious or senior dogs?

Yes — these are exactly the dogs a veterinary setting suits best. We work at a pace that fits the dog and stop when we need to instead of pushing through. Let us know when you book if your dog is nervous, elderly, or has a health condition, and if anxiety is severe, our veterinarians can discuss calming options with you.

Can I book grooming and a wellness exam at the same time?

Yes, and many owners do. Combining the two into a single trip is easier on nervous dogs and on your schedule, and it means anything noticed during the groom can be looked at by the vet the same day. Mention that you'd like both when you call.

How often should I have my dog groomed?

It depends on the coat. Many double-coated and long-haired breeds do well on a schedule of every four to eight weeks, while short-coated dogs may only need occasional baths and regular nail trims. We'll suggest a rhythm that fits your dog's coat and lifestyle at your first visit.

Do you offer Asian Fusion or other style cuts?

Yes — Asian Fusion, teddy-bear, and other finish styles are available on request. Bring a photo of the look you have in mind and we'll talk through what works for your dog's coat and comfort.

Ready to Book Your Dog's Groom?

Reach out and we'll answer your questions, give you a quote for your dog, and find a time that works. Anxious or senior dog? Just let us know — we'll plan around it.
