Dog Vet Near Pasadena — FAQ

How far is SPAH from Pasadena?

We're about 10 minutes from central Pasadena via the 110 freeway south. Our clinic at 3116 W Main St in Alhambra is easy to reach from the Pasadena area, with free street parking right out front.

Do you accept new patients from Pasadena?

Yes — we welcome new patients from Pasadena and the surrounding area. You can book online in under a minute, or call us at (626) 441-1314 and our team will get you scheduled.

What dog vaccines do you offer?

We offer all core dog vaccines including DHPP (distemper, hepatitis, parainfluenza, parvovirus) and rabies, as well as lifestyle vaccines like bordetella and leptospirosis based on your dog's risk factors.

How much does a dog wellness exam cost?

Our wellness exam fees are listed on our pricing page. We believe in full price transparency so you're never surprised by your bill.

Do you offer senior dog care?

Absolutely. For dogs seven years and older, we recommend more frequent wellness exams and comprehensive bloodwork panels to catch age-related conditions early — kidney disease, thyroid issues, heart conditions, and more.

Dog Veterinary Care Near Pasadena

If you live in Pasadena and are looking for a trustworthy dog vet, South Pasadena Animal Hospital is just a short drive away at 3116 W Main St in Alhambra. Many Pasadena dog owners choose SPAH for its independent, relationship-based approach to care — a welcome alternative to the large corporate veterinary chains that have expanded throughout the San Gabriel Valley in recent years.

Our team of veterinarians provides comprehensive dog care including wellness exams, core and lifestyle vaccinations, professional dental cleanings under anesthesia, spay and neuter surgery, allergy diagnosis and management, senior bloodwork panels, X-rays, and in-house lab testing. Whatever your dog needs, we aim to handle it in one place.

Pasadena dogs, like all dogs in the greater Los Angeles area, are commonly affected by seasonal environmental allergies. If your dog has been scratching, licking their paws, or developing recurring ear infections, our team can help identify the triggers and build a management plan tailored to the Southern California climate.
