Health Considerations for Pasadena Dogs

Pasadena's climate, landscape, and lifestyle create specific health challenges for local dogs. Our team is familiar with all of them.

Summer Heat & Hot Pavement

The San Gabriel Valley regularly hits 95–105°F in summer. Pasadena's sidewalks and asphalt can exceed 160°F on peak days — hot enough to blister paw pads within minutes. We counsel on safe exercise timing, signs of heat exhaustion, and paw pad care for active Pasadena dogs.

Foxtail Injuries from Trails

Foxtail grass is widespread on Eaton Canyon and other Pasadena-area trails. These barbed seed awns can embed in paws, ears, eyes, and nasal passages — sometimes requiring sedation to remove. We encourage trail owners to check their dogs after every hike.

Seasonal Allergies

Pasadena's abundant trees, ornamental gardens, and grass pollens make spring and fall particularly rough for allergy-prone dogs. Symptoms often show up as paw licking, face rubbing, hot spots, and chronic ear infections rather than sneezing. We build management plans that address root causes, not just symptoms.

Tick & Parasite Exposure

Dogs that frequent Hahamongna Watershed, the Arroyo Seco trail, or any of Pasadena's hillside parks face real tick exposure risk. We discuss appropriate parasite prevention at every wellness visit and can help you choose the right product for your dog's activity level.

Frequently Asked Questions

From Pasadena dog owners we hear regularly.

How far is South Pasadena Animal Hospital from Pasadena?

About 10 minutes via the 110 (Arroyo Seco Parkway) heading south. Exit at Meridian Ave or Mission Street in Alhambra. Free street parking is available directly in front of our clinic at 3116 W Main St.

Do you treat dogs that hike Eaton Canyon or use Brookside Park?

Yes. Active Pasadena dogs face specific risks including foxtail grass injuries, tick exposure on trails, overheating in summer heat, and paw pad injuries on hot pavement. We address all of these at wellness visits and sick visits — and we'll walk you through what to check after a trail hike.

What vaccines does my Pasadena dog need?

All dogs need core vaccines: DHPP and rabies, which is required by California law. Based on your dog's lifestyle — especially park and trail exposure in the Pasadena area — we may also recommend bordetella (kennel cough) and leptospirosis. We'll build the right schedule at your dog's wellness exam.

My dog has seasonal allergies. Can SPAH help?

Yes. The Pasadena area has significant seasonal pollen from trees, ornamental gardens, and grasses, especially in spring and fall. We regularly treat SGV dogs dealing with itching, recurring ear infections, paw licking, and skin inflammation from environmental allergies. We build management plans that address root causes, not just short-term symptom relief.

How do I get from Old Town Pasadena to SPAH?

Take Colorado Blvd west, head south on the 110 freeway, and exit at Meridian Ave. Turn right onto W Main St — we're at 3116, about 10 minutes from Old Town. The drive is direct and easy on the Arroyo Seco Parkway.

Do you see senior dogs?

Absolutely. For dogs 7 years and older, we recommend twice-yearly wellness exams with comprehensive bloodwork to monitor kidney function, thyroid levels, liver health, and other age-related conditions. Early detection is key to keeping senior dogs healthy longer — and it's something we take seriously at every visit.

How do I book an appointment?

Book online in under a minute through our patient portal, or call us at (626) 441-1314. New patients welcome — we recommend booking a few days in advance for the best availability.
