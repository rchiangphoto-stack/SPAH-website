Health Risks Specific to San Gabriel Dogs

Dense urban environments like San Gabriel come with their own set of health hazards. We see these regularly in our clinic and address them proactively.

Rodenticide Poisoning

Rat poison is widely used in urban SGV neighborhoods and restaurant corridors. Dogs can ingest it directly or by eating a poisoned rodent. Rodenticide affects blood clotting and can be fatal if not treated promptly. If you suspect exposure, contact a vet immediately — don't wait for symptoms to appear.

Urban Heat & Concrete Burns

San Gabriel's dense built environment absorbs and radiates heat. In summer, sidewalks and asphalt can reach temperatures that blister paw pads in minutes. We advise walking dogs before 9am or after 7pm during peak heat months, and we treat paw pad injuries that result from midday outings.

Ear Infections & Skin Allergies

Environmental allergies are extremely common in SGV dogs, driven by grasses, trees, and seasonal pollens. We frequently see San Gabriel dogs with recurring ear infections, paw licking, and skin inflammation. We identify triggers and build long-term management plans that go beyond one-time steroid treatments.

Dental Disease from Soft Food Diets

We see a high proportion of dogs in the SGV fed primarily wet or soft food, which accelerates tartar buildup. By age 3, most dogs show some degree of dental disease — and untreated, it contributes to kidney, heart, and liver problems. We check dental grade at every visit and offer professional cleanings under safe anesthesia.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from San Gabriel dog owners.

How far is South Pasadena Animal Hospital from San Gabriel?

San Gabriel and Alhambra share a border — we're typically 5–7 minutes from central San Gabriel via Valley Boulevard or Mission Road heading west. No freeway required. Free street parking is available directly in front of our clinic at 3116 W Main St.

Is rodenticide poisoning a real risk in San Gabriel?

Yes. Dense urban environments like San Gabriel have significant rodent control activity, and rodenticide is one of the most common toxin emergencies we see in the SGV. Dogs can be exposed directly or by eating a poisoned rodent. If you suspect exposure, contact a vet immediately — do not wait for symptoms, which can be delayed by days.

What vaccines does my San Gabriel dog need?

Core vaccines — DHPP and rabies (required by California law) — are needed for every dog. Depending on your dog's lifestyle, we may also recommend bordetella and leptospirosis. We'll discuss the right schedule at your dog's wellness exam and provide California license documentation for the rabies vaccine.

My dog keeps getting ear infections. Can you help?

Yes. Recurring ear infections are one of the most common reasons San Gabriel dogs come in. They're often a symptom of environmental allergies rather than a standalone problem. We diagnose the infection type, treat it, and work on identifying and managing the allergy trigger so you're not dealing with this every few months.

Do you offer dental cleanings for dogs?

Yes. Professional cleanings are performed under safe anesthesia and include scaling, polishing, and dental X-rays. We also perform extractions when needed. Dental disease affects most dogs by age 3 and is linked to heart, kidney, and liver problems if left untreated — it's something we check at every visit.

How do I book an appointment?

Book online in under a minute, or call (626) 441-1314. New patients from San Gabriel are always welcome. We recommend booking a few days ahead for the best appointment availability.
