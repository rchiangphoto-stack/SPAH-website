What to Expect at Your First Visit

El Monte is one of the larger cities in the San Gabriel Valley, and we've found that El Monte pet owners tend to be no-nonsense — they want straight answers and real care. That's exactly how we operate. When you arrive, our team will check you in promptly and make your pet as comfortable as possible before the exam.

El Monte Vet FAQ

Where is the closest vet to El Monte?

South Pasadena Animal Hospital is at 3116 W Main St in Alhambra, about 10 minutes west of El Monte via Valley Boulevard or Garvey Avenue. We're rated 4.5 stars and offer full-service care for both traditional and exotic pets.

Does SPAH treat exotic pets from El Monte?

Yes. We see reptiles (bearded dragons, geckos, turtles, ball pythons), birds (cockatiels, parrots, finches), rabbits, guinea pigs, hamsters, and other small mammals. If you've been struggling to find exotic vet care near El Monte, we're here for exactly that.

How do I get from El Monte to SPAH?

Take Valley Boulevard west through Rosemead into Alhambra, or take Garvey Avenue west. From the 10 freeway, exit at Fremont Avenue and head north to Main Street — we're at 3116 W Main St with street parking right out front.

How much does a vet visit cost near El Monte?

All fees are listed on our pricing page. We don't believe in surprise charges — you'll know the cost before anything is done. We'll always walk you through options and pricing before any treatment.

What are your hours?

Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM (with a 1:00–2:00 PM lunch break). Closed weekends. For after-hours emergencies, we recommend contacting a local emergency animal hospital.

Is SPAH affiliated with a corporate chain?

No. We're independently owned, led by Dr. Sylvia Chiang and Dr. Gina Navia. No corporate ownership, no chain quotas. Every recommendation is made based on what's right for your specific pet — nothing else.
