Temple City Vet FAQ

Where is the closest full-service vet to Temple City?

South Pasadena Animal Hospital is at 3116 W Main St in Alhambra, about 10 minutes west of Temple City. We're one of the only clinics in the area that treats both traditional pets and exotics, with in-house diagnostics, dental care, and surgery all on site.

Does SPAH treat exotic pets from Temple City?

Yes. We see reptiles (bearded dragons, geckos, turtles, snakes), birds (cockatiels, parrots), rabbits, guinea pigs, hamsters, and other small mammals. Temple City has a thriving community of exotic pet owners, and we're set up to serve them.

How do I get from Temple City to SPAH?

Take Las Tunas Drive west through San Gabriel directly into Alhambra — it becomes Main Street and our clinic is at 3116 W Main St. Valley Boulevard west is another easy route. From the 10 freeway, exit Fremont Avenue north. Street parking is right out front.

How much does a vet visit cost near Temple City?

All of our fees are listed on our pricing page. We believe in complete transparency — you'll know the cost before any treatment begins. No surprise charges at checkout.

Is SPAH open on weekends?

We're open Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 1:00 PM and 2:00 PM to 6:00 PM (closed for lunch 1–2 PM). We're closed on weekends. For after-hours emergencies, we recommend contacting a local emergency animal hospital.

Is SPAH a corporate vet chain?

No. We're independently owned and led by Dr. Sylvia Chiang and Dr. Gina Navia. Not part of any corporate group. That means decisions are always made with your pet's wellbeing in mind — not a chain-wide protocol.
